How the ATO treats this — general notes, not advice:
  • Capital gains: the same 50% CGT discount applies to US shares as Australian shares, as long as you've held them 12+ months as an Australian tax resident.
  • Dividends: the full (grossed-up) dividend — including any US tax withheld — must be declared as assessable income, converted to AUD.
  • Withholding rate: under the Australia–US tax treaty, US withholding on dividends is capped at 15% if a W-8BEN is on file with your broker; without one, the default is 30%.
  • No separate return: US tax withheld isn't a separate tax lodgment — you claim it back as a Foreign Income Tax Offset (FITO) within your one Australian return.

Source: ATO — Claiming a foreign income tax offset. General information only, not personalised tax advice — confirm your figures with a registered tax agent.
